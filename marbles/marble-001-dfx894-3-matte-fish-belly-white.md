### Visual Distinctiveness
The 亚光鱼肚白 (Matte Fish Belly White) name references the 鱼肚白 classical Chinese aesthetic — the pale, slightly translucent white seen on a fish's belly, used to describe the most prized white marble tone in Chinese interior culture. This film captures the Statuario cloud vein layout: most of the surface is pure open white, with the grey vein drama localised to one quadrant. The matte finish is the correct choice — gloss on pure white marble film immediately reads as plastic. Matte gives it the dry stone surface quality that specification-grade buyers expect.

## B4 · Scoring — 7 Dimensions

### D1 · Design Fidelity (Weight: 25%) — Score: 4 / 5
Good execution of the Bianco Statuario cloud vein character. The pure white base is accurate; the asymmetric grey cloud mass in one quadrant with an open field is the correct Statuario DNA. The faint secondary wisps add geological authenticity. Loses one point because the grey vein tone is slightly flat and uniform — natural Statuario has more internal density variation within the cloud, with lighter feathering at the vein periphery and darker concentration at the core. The film's print process compresses this tonal range.

### D2 · Colour-Palette Fit (Weight: 20%) — Score: 5 / 5
Pure white + grey vein fills India's Essential Slot #1 — the single most demanded marble colour combination across every consumer segment, every city, every room type. Maximum commercial relevance. Perfect score.

### D3 · Veining Drama & Naturalism (Weight: 18%) — Score: 3 / 5
The drama is restrained and asymmetric rather than bold and sweeping. At room scale, the vein cloud creates a quiet geological statement — elegant but not immediately arresting. This is a sophisticate's choice, not a showroom floor showstopper. Loses two points because: (1) the grey is not very dark — a bolder charcoal would create more contrast drama; (2) the vein is heavily one-sided, which reads beautifully on a single surface but creates an obvious repeat across multiple panels.

### D4 · Surface Finish Quality (Weight: 12%) — Score: 4 / 5
Matte (亚光) is the correct finish for white marble film in the 2024–27 Indian premium market. Avoids the "cheap white bathroom tile" read that gloss white marble creates. On-trend. Consistent across the field. Loses one point because without EIR emboss depth, the surface is purely visual — touch reveals flat film rather than the cool, dense feel of stone.

### D5 · Application Versatility (Weight: 10%) — Score: 5 / 5
White marble film is maximally application-versatile. Works on every furniture surface: wardrobe shutters, modular kitchen cabinets, TV unit panels, study table surfaces, bathroom vanity fronts, pooja unit doors. Perfect score — matched only by the whitest stone panels in the catalog.

### D6 · Tactile Texture (Weight: 8%) — Score: 2 / 5
Flat film print — no EIR (embossed-in-register) surface texture visible. The matte finish adds some surface character but the haptic experience is smooth film, not stone. A leathered or open-pore matte variant with micro-emboss would significantly improve this score.

### D7 · Trend Alignment (Weight: 7%) — Score: 4 / 5
Matte white Statuario is squarely in the 2024–27 Indian premium market direction. The shift from gloss white marble to matte white marble is the single biggest finish trend in the Indian modular furniture and wardrobe market. This film is correctly specified for that trend. Loses one point because the vein drama is understated — bolder Calacatta interpretations are gaining share alongside Statuario.
