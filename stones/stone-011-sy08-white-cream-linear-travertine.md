### Visual Distinctiveness
SY08 is the closest panel in this batch to the #1 India collection essential: **Classic White + Grey Vein (Carrara-style)**. The white-cream base with multiple parallel vertical grey-brown veins fills this highest-volume slot with a travertine interpretation rather than a marble interpretation. The distinction matters: Carrara marble has sweeping, cloud-like, non-directional veining; this panel's dense parallel vertical vein system is travertine/limestone linear layering. Both reference families are premium and credible; the linear-vein travertine version is particularly strong for 2024-2027 because it aligns with the travertine trend wave.

## B4 · Scoring — 7 Dimensions

### D1 · Design Fidelity (Weight: 25%) — Score: 4 / 5
Strong white travertine execution. The dense parallel grey vein system on a warm white-cream base is an accurate reference to Silver Travertine or Bianco Travertino. The vein density and grey-brown colour are well-calibrated. The satin finish is appropriate. Loses one point because the linear vein system is very regular (parallel and evenly spaced at similar width) — natural travertine shows more vein width variation and occasional diagonal deviation.

### D2 · Colour-Palette Fit (Weight: 20%) — Score: 5 / 5
White/cream with grey veining is India's single most demanded stone colour combination — it fills Essential Slot #1 (Classic White + Grey Vein). This is the colour that every buyer considers first, that every collection must have, and that drives the highest total volume. Perfect score.

### D3 · Veining Drama & Naturalism (Weight: 18%) — Score: 4 / 5
The dense parallel vertical vein system creates a strong visual rhythm — not the explosive branching drama of stone-004, but the refined architectural order of linear travertine. The multiple closely-spaced veins create a surface that reads as complex and rich rather than simple. Scores 4/5 — below the branching vein systems in drama but above single-vein panels in visual complexity.

### D4 · Surface Finish Quality (Weight: 12%) — Score: 4 / 5
Satin finish is appropriate for the white-cream category — it provides a slight warmth-enhancing sheen without creating the glare and fingerprint problems of high gloss. A matte variant would score equally well and potentially better for the premium Japandi segment. Consistent and well-executed.

### D5 · Application Versatility (Weight: 10%) — Score: 5 / 5
White cream stone with grey veining is the most application-universal stone surface in existence. Works in every room, for every consumer segment, across all geographies. Perfect versatility score — matched only by stone-008 and stone-009.

### D6 · Tactile Texture (Weight: 8%) — Score: 3 / 5
Satin finish surface — refined but not deeply tactile. The linear veins create visual texture but the flat satin surface doesn't add significant haptic depth. An open-pore matte variant of this panel would score 4-5/5 and would be a very compelling premium proposition.

### D7 · Trend Alignment (Weight: 7%) — Score: 4 / 5
White travertine with linear grey veining is the travertine trend's most accessible expression — broader than the warm buff variants, because the white-cream base makes it acceptable to buyers from both the "I want marble" (white reference) and "I want travertine" (linear vein reference) decision paths. Strong 2024-2027 alignment.
