## B2 · Visual Profile

| Attribute | Description |
|---|---|
| **Base Tone** | Mid-cool grey — approximately Pantone Cool Grey 8–10 range; neither blue-grey nor warm taupe |
| **Tonal Variation** | Moderate cloud/mist variation — lighter zones at ~40% grey, darker zones at ~65% grey |
| **Primary Vein System** | Bold branching white veins, 3–8mm width, flowing diagonally across the panel — classic Bardiglio-branch pattern |
| **Secondary Vein System** | Dense dark hairline fissure network — 0.3–0.8mm lines criss-crossing in multiple directions, creating a unique "cracked ice" sub-texture |
| **Figure / Character** | High drama — the dual-vein system (bold white + dark fissure matrix) is extremely rare and highly distinctive |
| **Surface Texture (visual)** | Smooth to semi-polished; fissure lines create the illusion of genuine stone depth even on flat panels |
| **Directional Flow** | Veins run primarily diagonal (bottom-left to upper-right), which requires careful panel orientation during installation |

### Visual Distinctiveness
The dual-vein composition — large-scale bold white branching veins overlaid with a fine dark hairline fissure network — is this panel's most compelling differentiator. In the Indian grey marble category, most panels show either bold veining OR fine texture; combining both at this density is extremely unusual. This creates a "depth illusion" that passes inspection far more convincingly than single-vein grey panels.

## B6 · Application-Space Analysis

### Primary Applications (Must-Stock)
1. **TV / Media Feature Wall** — The #1 use case for grey marble in urban India. The dual-vein system creates "nature behind the screen" depth without competing with content.
2. **Foyer / Entry Statement** — Mid-grey reads as sophisticated restraint; bold veining delivers the "arrival moment" that HNI clients seek.
3. **Bedroom Headboard Feature Wall** — Cool grey creates the sleep-conducive calm that contemporary bedroom design demands; bold white veins add luxury without excitement.

### Secondary Applications (Include in Range)
4. **Dining Accent Wall** — Pairs superbly with warm timber dining furniture (the classic cool-stone + warm-wood contrast).
5. **Luxury Bathroom Full-Surround** — Grey + white palette is the dominant contemporary bathroom aesthetic in premium Indian projects.
6. **Commercial Lobby / Hospitality** — Bardiglio-type grey is the standard specification in 4–5 star lobbies.

### Avoid
- **Pooja units / spiritual spaces** — Grey is culturally neutral-to-negative in traditional Hindu contexts; warm white or cream strongly preferred.
- **Children's rooms** — Cool grey reads as cold and clinical in residential child spaces.

### Natural Pairings in Indian Interiors
| Element | Recommended Pairing |
|---|---|
| Timber | Warm walnut (strongest contrast), medium teak (grounded) |
| Metal accents | Brass (gold warmth balances cool grey), matte black |
| Sofa / upholstery | Warm ivory, deep navy, charcoal |
| Floor material | Warm sand-tone porcelain, pale wood plank |
| Lighting | Warm 2700–3000K CCT to counter cool grey with warmth |

## Summary Verdict

**stone-001 Silver Grey Marble** is the most capable grey panel assessed to date. The dual-vein system — bold white branches overlaid with a fine dark hairline fissure network — creates a depth of naturalism that single-vein grey panels cannot replicate. It fills the essential Grey slot in any India-facing stone panel collection and is particularly well positioned for the high-growth Aspirational Professional and Design Millennial segments in Mumbai, Bengaluru, and Pune.

The single most impactful improvement: **develop a honed matte variant**. The India luxury market is shifting from high-gloss to matte/honed rapidly (fastest-growing finish tier), and this panel's visual language translates even more powerfully in a matte finish where the fissure texture becomes tactile rather than purely visual.
