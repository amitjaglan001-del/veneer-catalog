### Visual Distinctiveness
SY20 is the warmest-toned panel in the batch — the pinkish-blush undertone is distinctive and unusual. The heavy open-pore texture combined with the pink-beige tone creates a material that references Rajasthani sandstone and Dholpur pink limestone — natural stone materials deeply embedded in Indian architectural heritage (Rajasthan forts, Lutyens Delhi, Agra sandstone). This cultural resonance is a genuine commercial opportunity in the North India market but a limitation elsewhere where the pink tone reads as overly feminine or dated.

## B4 · Scoring — 7 Dimensions

### D1 · Design Fidelity (Weight: 25%) — Score: 3 / 5
Convincing as a textured limestone or sandstone surface — the pink-cream tone and rough texture together create an accurate "pink sandstone" reference. However, like stone-003, the fidelity is to a vernacular stone archetype rather than a prestigious marble reference. The scoring system rewards prestige stone references; sandstone/limestone reads as less premium. +1 for the cultural Indian heritage reference (Rajasthani pink sandstone).

### D2 · Colour-Palette Fit (Weight: 20%) — Score: 4 / 5
The pink-blush undertone is the most distinctive colour move in this batch. In North India (Delhi, Agra, Rajasthan aesthetic influence), pink-beige stone is deeply familiar and coveted — Dholpur stone appears in Humayun's Tomb, New Delhi's Central Vista, and countless Rajasthani havelis. However, pink undertones read as less versatile than pure cream in South India and urban contemporary markets. Score reflects strong regional appeal with limited national reach.

### D3 · Veining Drama & Naturalism (Weight: 18%) — Score: 1 / 5
No veining. Texture-first panel — same structural weakness as stone-003 on this dimension. The heavy surface relief compensates in other dimensions but D3 score reflects absence of veining.

### D4 · Surface Finish Quality (Weight: 12%) — Score: 4 / 5
Matte open-pore finish is perfectly matched to the sandstone/textured stone aesthetic. Any gloss on this surface would immediately destroy the natural stone illusion. The matte execution appears consistent and authentic.

### D5 · Application Versatility (Weight: 10%) — Score: 3 / 5
Heavy texture + pink undertone restricts application. Best as: feature wall accent, foyer, outdoor-to-indoor transition, heritage-inspired projects. Not suitable for: kitchen shutters, bathrooms (maintenance), wardrobe surfaces. The pink tone further restricts universality.

### D6 · Tactile Texture (Weight: 8%) — Score: 5 / 5
The highest tactile quality of the warm panels in this batch — second only to stone-003 (Classical Rock) overall. The open-pore or embossed surface creates genuine haptic depth that dramatically differentiates from smooth marble panels. The shadow created by the surface relief under raking light is a significant visual and spatial quality that changes the room.

### D7 · Trend Alignment (Weight: 7%) — Score: 4 / 5
Textured natural surfaces and biophilic materials are strong 2024-2027 trends. The pink-beige additionally connects with the broader "warm earth tones" and "raw natural materials" design movement. Slightly niche because the pink tone is not a mainstream design trend but is strongly rising in the heritage-contemporary and boutique hospitality sectors.

## Summary Verdict

**stone-010 SY20** is the collection's heritage wildcard — a panel that scores poorly on the framework's most heavily weighted dimensions (no veining: D3 = 1/5; moderate fidelity: D1 = 3/5) but delivers the richest tactile quality (D6 = 5/5) of all warm panels and carries unique cultural resonance with India's sandstone architectural heritage. Its commercial opportunity is **North India heritage revival projects, boutique hospitality, foyer feature walls, and design-forward clients who actively seek differentiation.** Not a volume SKU; a signature accent for the right brief.
