### Visual Distinctiveness
SY130 is the character-rich sibling to SY134's elegance. Where SY134 is refined and ivory, SY130 is warmer, richer, and more earthy — closer to Noce Travertine or Walnut Travertine. The buff-golden warmth and the distribution of grey cloud patches create a surface with the most visual complexity among the warm panels in this batch. This is the panel that rewards prolonged looking — there is always more tonal detail to discover. In Indian residential, this is the "personality" version of the warm cream essential.

## B4 · Scoring — 7 Dimensions

### D1 · Design Fidelity (Weight: 25%) — Score: 4 / 5
Strong Noce/Walnut Travertine reference — the buff warmth, grey clouds, and directional tonal flow accurately capture the DNA of the most characterful travertine variants. The grey patches are a signature of natural travertine (impurity inclusions in the calcium carbonate matrix) that add significant authenticity. Loses one point because the vein/flow system is diffuse rather than structurally defined — at room scale it may read as warm texture rather than stone.

### D2 · Colour-Palette Fit (Weight: 20%) — Score: 5 / 5
The richest, warmest panel in the batch — and warm is exactly what India wants. The buff-golden cream tone with grey character patches creates a surface that resonates across North India (Delhi, Lucknow, Jaipur — all warm-tone dominant), South India, Tier-2, and premium residential. The richer warmth of SY130 vs. SY134 means it connects more strongly with heritage buyers (teak/sheesham affinity — warm golden wood equivalent in stone). Perfect score.

### D3 · Veining Drama & Naturalism (Weight: 18%) — Score: 3 / 5
Like SY134, the drama is in the overall texture and tonal richness rather than defined vein lines. The grey cloud patches add visual interest and naturalism. Scores same as SY134 (3/5) — the panel's strength is in colour and character, not in veining drama per the scoring framework's definition.

### D4 · Surface Finish Quality (Weight: 12%) — Score: 4 / 5
Matte finish is correctly matched to this warm buff tone — the warmth needs to breathe rather than be reflected. Consistent across the field. Well-executed for the warm travertine aesthetic.

### D5 · Application Versatility (Weight: 10%) — Score: 5 / 5
Warm buff cream works across all room types, all consumer segments, all geographic markets. Even more versatile than SY134 for traditional and Tier-2 markets because the richer warmth is more aligned with traditional Indian material preferences (teak, teakwood, sandstone). Perfect versatility.

### D6 · Tactile Texture (Weight: 8%) — Score: 4 / 5
Better texture score than SY134 — the richer buff tone and slightly rougher matte surface translate into a more tactile quality. The grey patches suggest surface depth and variation that approaches the natural travertine haptic experience.

### D7 · Trend Alignment (Weight: 7%) — Score: 4 / 5
Travertine, particularly the warm Noce/Walnut variants, is strongly aligned with the 2024-2027 Indian premium market direction. The earthy buff tone additionally connects with the biophilic and "warm natural materials" trend running parallel to Japandi.
