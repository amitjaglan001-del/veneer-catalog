### Visual Distinctiveness
The warm grey-taupe occupies a genuinely useful market position: it bridges the gap between warm cream (stone-008, stone-009) and cool grey (stone-001, stone-006) palettes. The "greige" tone appeals to buyers who want a neutral that doesn't commit fully to either warm or cool. The single dramatic vein gives it one strong focal element. Its risk: neither the warmth of cream nor the drama of cool grey — it can feel like a compromise rather than a deliberate choice.

## B4 · Scoring — 7 Dimensions

### D1 · Design Fidelity (Weight: 25%) — Score: 3 / 5
Competent greige limestone reference. The warm grey base is well-calibrated and the diagonal vein has naturalistic flow. Penalised for a single vein system — real greige limestone stones (Fior di Bosco, Gris du Midi) have more complex multi-directional vein networks. The SPC substrate gives the panel physical structural authenticity even if the design is somewhat simplified.

### D2 · Colour-Palette Fit (Weight: 20%) — Score: 3 / 5
Warm grey-taupe is a growing but still secondary preference in India. Its strength is cross-category appeal (bridges warm and cool preferences). Its weakness is that in a market where buyers tend to commit to either warm or cool, the greige tone can read as indecisive. Strong for the transitional buyer (30–45, design-aware but not design-led).

### D3 · Veining Drama & Naturalism (Weight: 18%) — Score: 3 / 5
A single diagonal vein is a moderate design statement — more interesting than no veins (stone-002, stone-010) but well below the multi-vein drama of stone-001, stone-004, or stone-011. The vein width and flow look naturalistic, which is the positive. The scarcity of the vein system leaves the field — a warm grey field — as the primary surface read, which is pleasant but not premium.

### D4 · Surface Finish Quality (Weight: 12%) — Score: 4 / 5
Semi-matte finish is well-aligned with the premium Indian market trend (2024-2027). The SPC substrate provides better dimensional stability than PVC, which is a practical advantage. Surface appears consistent and well-calibrated for the warm grey tone.

### D5 · Application Versatility (Weight: 10%) — Score: 3 / 5
Warm grey-taupe is versatile within its register — works for bedroom headboards, living room feature walls, study walls, and bathroom. More limited than pure warm cream (stone-008) but broader than dark panels. Medium versatility.

### D6 · Tactile Texture (Weight: 8%) — Score: 4 / 5
Visible surface texture and granularity — the semi-matte semi-textured surface of this SPC panel gives it better haptic quality than the smooth marble-look panels. The stone field character comes through visually and will translate into tactile depth.

### D7 · Trend Alignment (Weight: 7%) — Score: 3 / 5
Greige/warm grey is a moderate 2024-2027 trend in India — not as powerful as travertine (strong upward) or cool grey Japandi (strong). Positioned in the "stable and safe" middle of the trend matrix rather than on any strong vector.
