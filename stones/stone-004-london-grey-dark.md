### Visual Distinctiveness
This is the single highest-drama panel in the batch. The bold white vein system on a near-black charcoal base is a reference to Nero Marquina — one of the most coveted dark marbles globally. In the Indian market, this type of panel fills the "Dark Accent" essential slot and generates the strongest initial visual impact. Its challenge is that dark stones have limited room-scale application in Indian homes — but for the specific use cases where they work (TV wall, luxury bathroom, commercial lobby), they are the most memorable SKU in the range.

## B4 · Scoring — 7 Dimensions

### D1 · Design Fidelity (Weight: 25%) — Score: 4 / 5
Strong execution of dark marble with bold white veining. The vein branching pattern follows natural geological logic. The charcoal base tone is accurately calibrated — neither too blue nor too warm. Loses one point because at image scale the vein edges appear slightly hard (real Nero Marquina has feathered vein transitions); also the hairline network could be denser to fully replicate natural stone depth.

### D2 · Colour-Palette Fit (Weight: 20%) — Score: 3 / 5
Dark charcoal is the 4th-preference colour in Indian residential (behind white > cream > grey). It has strong appeal in premium urban segments but is a niche purchase for mass market. Full commercial realisation requires a design-forward buyer or specific accent application context. Score reflects real market position — premium but restricted volume.

### D3 · Veining Drama & Naturalism (Weight: 18%) — Score: 5 / 5
Maximum veining drama. The bold white-on-charcoal contrast is the highest-contrast vein configuration possible in stone panels. Veins flow with naturalistic branching logic. The secondary hairline network adds the depth layer that separates good dark panels from great ones. This is the standout veining performance in the entire batch.

### D4 · Surface Finish Quality (Weight: 12%) — Score: 3 / 5
Semi-gloss finish on a dark panel is commercially reasonable (gloss is still dominant in darker stones for Indian market), but the fastest-growing premium buyers increasingly want honed or matte dark stone. Gloss on charcoal also shows fingerprints heavily — a practical concern for wall panels. A matte variant would score 4/5.

### D5 · Application Versatility (Weight: 10%) — Score: 2 / 5
Dark panels have structurally limited application in Indian homes — too heavy for continuous coverage, unsuitable for small spaces, culturally complex in traditional homes. However, within its specific use cases it is a dominant performer. Scores 2/5 because those use cases are narrow relative to lighter panels.

### D6 · Tactile Texture (Weight: 8%) — Score: 3 / 5
Semi-polished flat surface. No significant embossed texture. The bold veins create strong visual texture but the haptic experience is limited. A brushed or leathered dark variant would be significantly stronger.

### D7 · Trend Alignment (Weight: 7%) — Score: 4 / 5
Dark stone accent walls are a strong 2024-2027 trend in premium Indian interiors — particularly for TV walls (the #1 application) where dark panels create theatrical contrast behind the screen. The London Grey branding suggests awareness of the contemporary international market. Slightly below maximum because the dark palette is trend-forward rather than mainstream.
