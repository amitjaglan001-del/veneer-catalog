### Visual Distinctiveness
A gentle, approachable warm cream marble look. The "CE Effect" label suggests a ceramic or porcelain tile-format with a stone surface effect rather than a large-format wall panel. The understated veining makes it the most universally acceptable surface in this batch — the design equivalent of a neutral wardrobe staple. Its weakness is the same as its strength: it lacks drama that premium buyers increasingly demand.

## B4 · Scoring — 7 Dimensions

### D1 · Design Fidelity (Weight: 25%) — Score: 3 / 5
Competent execution of warm cream marble aesthetic. Vein density and tone are accurate but minimal — reads as "safe" rather than "convincing." No bold feature veins means it passes inspection at 3m but does not reward closer inspection. -1 for low vein authenticity. -1 for smooth surface that lacks the granular depth of real Crema Marfil.

### D2 · Colour-Palette Fit (Weight: 20%) — Score: 5 / 5
Warm cream is the #2 most demanded colour in Indian residential market (behind white-grey). This tone resonates across all regions — North India (Delhi, Lucknow, Surat), South India (Chennai warm-preference), and Tier-2 cities. Perfect score for colour fit.

### D3 · Veining Drama & Naturalism (Weight: 18%) — Score: 2 / 5
Very low drama veining. The surface reads as almost plain cream rather than a veined marble. Scores low because the veining system — the most important differentiator in the stone panel category — is nearly absent. Buyers who want plain cream can find plain cream; this doesn't earn premium pricing through its veining.

### D4 · Surface Finish Quality (Weight: 12%) — Score: 4 / 5
Semi-gloss/satin finish is appropriate for the warm-cream category in Indian market. North India and Tier-2 markets still prefer gloss and semi-gloss for warm stones. Finish quality appears consistent from image.

### D5 · Application Versatility (Weight: 10%) — Score: 4 / 5
Warm cream works in almost all applications — TV walls, foyer, bedroom headboard, kitchen, bathroom. Loses one point because the low drama means it underperforms as a feature wall (the #1 use case where visual impact matters most).

### D6 · Tactile Texture (Weight: 8%) — Score: 2 / 5
Smooth, flat surface. No embossed texture visible. The surface does not add haptic value. Lowest texture score in the batch for a marble-look.

### D7 · Trend Alignment (Weight: 7%) — Score: 3 / 5
Crema Marfil-style is a perennial classic — stable demand, not declining, but also not riding any 2024-2027 trend wave. Travertine (stone-008, stone-011) is the warm-stone trend story right now. This panel is trend-neutral.

## Summary Verdict

**stone-002** fills the Warm Beige/Cream essential slot at the entry level — it is the safest, most universally acceptable surface in this batch. Its core problem is that it substitutes safety for distinctiveness. In a market increasingly driven by feature wall drama (TV wall, foyer headboard), a panel with this little veining competes on price rather than design. **Best positioned as a bathroom, kitchen, or Pooja room surface rather than a hero feature wall panel.**
