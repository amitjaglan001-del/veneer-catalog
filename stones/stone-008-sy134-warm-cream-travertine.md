### Visual Distinctiveness
SY134 is the gentler, more refined of the two INTCO warm cream travertines (vs. SY130 stone-009). The cream-ivory base is softer and more ivory-white than SY130's richer buff, and the grey cloud distribution is subtler. This creates a surface that reads as elegant and approachable rather than characterful and rustic. It fills the Crema Marfil / Warm Cream essential slot with a travertine twist that is significantly more on-trend than a standard cream marble.

## B4 · Scoring — 7 Dimensions

### D1 · Design Fidelity (Weight: 25%) — Score: 4 / 5
Strong execution of Roman Travertine / Travertino Classico reference. The vertical linear banding, soft grey clouds, and cream-ivory base tone accurately capture the visual DNA of classic Italian travertine. Loses one point because the subtlety of the veining means it could read as "plain cream with patches" rather than a deliberate travertine reference at casual inspection from 3m+.

### D2 · Colour-Palette Fit (Weight: 20%) — Score: 5 / 5
Warm cream/ivory is India's most demanded stone colour. This tone resonates across all consumer segments and geographies — traditional (Delhi, Ahmedabad, Tier-2), modern (Mumbai, Bengaluru), and cross-category residential + commercial. Perfect score.

### D3 · Veining Drama & Naturalism (Weight: 18%) — Score: 3 / 5
Subtle veining is the design tradeoff of this panel — it gains universal palatability but loses the drama that drives premium purchase decisions. The grey cloud patches and linear banding are naturalistic and pleasant but do not create the "first impression impact" that stronger vein systems deliver. Middle score for medium drama.

### D4 · Surface Finish Quality (Weight: 12%) — Score: 4 / 5
Matte/satin finish is the right choice for warm cream travertine — it softens the ivory tone into something luxurious rather than clinical (which high gloss cream often becomes). On-trend finish. Appears consistent and well-calibrated.

### D5 · Application Versatility (Weight: 10%) — Score: 5 / 5
Warm cream travertine in matte/satin finish is the most universally applicable surface in the batch. Works in every room type, across all consumer segments, and pairs with virtually any palette. Perfect versatility score.

### D6 · Tactile Texture (Weight: 8%) — Score: 3 / 5
Moderate tactile quality — the matte surface has some texture depth but the clouds and banding are primarily visual rather than haptic effects. The open-pore travertine surface that makes natural stone so tactile is only partially replicated here.

### D7 · Trend Alignment (Weight: 7%) — Score: 4 / 5
Travertine is the dominant stone trend 2024-2027. This panel's design language (cream + linear banding + grey clouds + matte) is a textbook travertine execution. Strong trend alignment across all urban Indian markets.
