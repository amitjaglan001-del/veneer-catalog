## B2 · Visual Profile

| Attribute | Description |
|---|---|
| **Base Tone** | Warm greige — approximately Pantone Warm Grey 3–4 with cream undertone; sits between warm cream and cool grey; no blue cast |
| **Tonal Variation** | Moderate — slightly warmer cream zones and cooler grey zones float across the field; gentle cloud variation |
| **Primary Vein System** | White/cream flowing veins — 5–20mm wide, running predominantly vertical with organic branching and lateral spread; soft-edged, naturalistic flow |
| **Secondary Vein System** | Copper/reddish-brown hairline veins — very fine (0.5–2mm), scattered across the entire field; quasi-random directional scatter creates a "geological scratch" pattern distinct from the primary white veins |
| **Figure / Character** | High complexity — the interplay of three tonal layers (warm greige field + white primary veins + copper hairline network) creates a surface with exceptional visual depth |
| **Surface Texture (visual)** | Semi-matte / satin — refined surface; the satin finish enhances the warm undertone |
| **Directional Flow** | Vertical dominant (primary veins) + scattered (copper hairlines) — the copper scatter prevents the panel from having a single forced orientation; versatile installation |

### Visual Distinctiveness
This panel's defining feature is the **copper/reddish-brown hairline vein network** — a geological characteristic of Diana Royal and Fior di Pesco Carnico marbles that sets them apart from every other warm grey marble. In the Indian market, the copper vein system is particularly potent because it resonates directly with the dominant premium hardware trend: brushed brass and copper fixtures are the #1 metal accent in Indian luxury residential projects (2023-2027). A stone panel that visually echoes the copper tones of the hardware creates instant visual coherence — and this panel does exactly that.

## B4 · Scoring — 7 Dimensions

### D1 · Design Fidelity (Weight: 25%) — Score: 4 / 5
Strong execution of the Diana Royal / Fior di Pesco Carnico marble character. The warm greige base, the white flowing primary veins, and the copper hairline secondary network together constitute an accurate three-layer geological portrait. Diana Royal is sourced from the Apuan Alps (Carrara region) — the same mountain range as Statuario and Calacatta — giving it prestige provenance that resonates with specification architects. Loses one point because the copper vein density appears slightly uniform at panel scale — natural Fior di Pesco has more clustering variation in the copper hairlines.

### D2 · Colour-Palette Fit (Weight: 20%) — Score: 4 / 5
Warm greige occupies a commercially strong position: warm enough to appeal to India's dominant warm-tone preference, modern enough to satisfy the contemporary design segment. The copper undertone — unlike the cooler grey panels — actively pairs with the most dominant premium hardware trend in Indian interiors (brass and copper accents). This synergy between stone colour and hardware choice is a genuine commercial advantage. Scores below 5/5 because the greige tone is less universally embraced than pure warm cream in Tier-2 and North India traditional markets.

### D3 · Veining Drama & Naturalism (Weight: 18%) — Score: 4 / 5
The dual vein system generates significant visual complexity — the white primary veins provide the structural drama (organic branching flow) while the copper hairlines provide the intimate geological detail. Together they create a surface that reads differently at 5m (white vein architecture) and at 0.5m (copper hairline micro-detail). This multi-scale visual engagement is rare and premium. Loses one point because the primary white vein could be more dramatic (larger scale branching) to push the first-impression impact to 5/5 level.

### D4 · Surface Finish Quality (Weight: 12%) — Score: 4 / 5
Semi-matte/satin is the correct finish for this stone — it warms the greige base without creating glare, and allows the copper vein colour to read accurately (gloss would wash out the copper). On-trend for 2024-2027. Consistent across the slab.

### D5 · Application Versatility (Weight: 10%) — Score: 4 / 5
Warm greige + copper is broadly applicable. The warm undertone means it works in traditional-leaning spaces; the grey component means it works in contemporary spaces; the copper veins make it a natural complement to brass hardware in both contexts. Strong for feature walls, foyer, bedroom headboard, dining. Loses one point because the copper colour is assertive enough that it doesn't recede in the background — spaces with conflicting accent colours (chrome, steel-blue) may clash.

### D6 · Tactile Texture (Weight: 8%) — Score: 3 / 5
Semi-matte/satin surface — refined and consistent but not deeply tactile. The surface quality is sophisticated without being strongly haptic. A leathered or open-pore variant would significantly elevate this panel.

### D7 · Trend Alignment (Weight: 7%) — Score: 4 / 5
Warm grey with copper/rose veining is a strong 2024-2027 design direction in Indian premium interiors, driven by: (1) the dominance of brass/copper hardware; (2) the "warm luxury" palette shift away from cool neutrals; (3) Neo-Classical revival that embraces warm veined marbles over cool grey. The copper vein synergy with hardware is the most direct trend connection in this collection.

## B9 · Collection Positioning

This panel fills a gap that no other panel in the catalog covers: **the warm grey-beige with copper accent veining**. It bridges:
- Essential Slot #4 (Warm Cream) — the base tone is warmer than cool grey
- Essential Slot #3 (Grey) — the grey component satisfies the grey marble demand
- Copper trend — the vein system connects to the dominant Indian hardware palette

It is effectively a **bridge SKU** that appeals to buyers who want neither "safe cream" nor "cool grey" — a genuine design statement within the warm spectrum. Best positioned as the **premium upgrade** in the warm stone sub-collection, one tier above SY134 and SY130 in design sophistication.

## Summary Verdict

**stone-012** earns 78/100 — the same score as stone-001 (Silver Grey Marble), but for different reasons. Where stone-001 wins on dramatic dual-grey vein complexity, stone-012 wins on market relevance through the copper-brass hardware synergy and warm greige tone. For Delhi NCR and Mumbai's premium residential market in 2024-2027, this is the most contextually precise panel in the collection — the one that a designer will specify because it makes every brass fixture in the room look intentional.

**Priority fix:** Develop an open-pore matte or leathered variant — the copper veins would become even more dimensional and tactile under a textured finish, elevating this panel from 78 to 82-84.
