## B4 · Scoring — 7 Dimensions

### D1 · Design Fidelity (Weight: 25%) — Score: 4 / 5
Convincing reference to light grey marble with dramatic crack character. The cool blue-grey base is well-calibrated and the single crack vein has realistic width variation and edge character. Penalised half a point: very light stones require extremely accurate colour control to avoid looking "cheap white plastic" — need to verify in person that the cool grey holds at close range.

### D2 · Colour-Palette Fit (Weight: 20%) — Score: 3 / 5
Very light cool grey with blue cast is the most niche colour in this batch for the Indian mass market. India's warm-tone preference dominates; cool ice-grey is firmly a design-forward premium urban choice. However, within that premium Japandi/architectural segment, this tone is highly coveted. Scores 3/5 because the colour, while excellent for its niche, has the narrowest mass-market appeal of the batch.

### D3 · Veining Drama & Naturalism (Weight: 18%) — Score: 4 / 5
The single crack vein approach creates high drama through stark contrast rather than vein complexity. The vein reads as a geological fracture event — authentic and distinctive. Penalised one point because a single vein system creates a repeating-pattern risk at room scale (the vein position will obviously repeat across multiple panels in a run without large-scale randomisation).

### D4 · Surface Finish Quality (Weight: 12%) — Score: 4 / 5
Matte/satin finish is the correct choice for this cool light tone — gloss would amplify the blue cast and risk looking clinical. The fine-texture matte finish adds surface sophistication. On-trend for 2024-2027.

### D5 · Application Versatility (Weight: 10%) — Score: 3 / 5
Cool blue-grey restricts versatility — doesn't pair with warm-traditional interiors, limited in South India warm-preference market. Within contemporary urban contexts, it has strong application range. The plank format (3000×250mm) means a wall requires multiple planks with visible joins — different installation aesthetic from large slab panels.

### D6 · Tactile Texture (Weight: 8%) — Score: 3 / 5
Fine matte texture — refined and well-executed but not deeply tactile. The surface is sophisticated rather than dramatic in the haptic register.

### D7 · Trend Alignment (Weight: 7%) — Score: 4 / 5
The lightest, coolest panel in the batch is strongly aligned with the Japandi and Quiet Luxury trends driving premium urban Indian design in 2024-2027. Particularly resonant in the Bengaluru and Pune markets.
