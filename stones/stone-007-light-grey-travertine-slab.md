### Visual Distinctiveness
This is the most architecturally sophisticated panel in the batch — a large-format slab with multiple parallel flowing white veins on a warm-light-grey ground, executed in an open-pore matte finish. The travertine/limestone reference is currently (2024-2027) the dominant stone trend in premium global and Indian interior design, driven by the shift from polished marble to raw-natural stone aesthetics. The large slab format and open-pore matte surface together create a "real stone wall" illusion that smaller or glossier panels cannot achieve.

## B4 · Scoring — 7 Dimensions

### D1 · Design Fidelity (Weight: 25%) — Score: 4 / 5
Very convincing Silver Travertine or Grigio Noce reference. The multiple parallel vein system, warm-grey base, and open-pore texture together create strong geological authenticity. The large slab format is critical — this design only reads convincingly at scale. Loses one point because at close inspection, the parallel vein regularity (veins are evenly spaced) may read as printed pattern rather than natural stone randomness.

### D2 · Colour-Palette Fit (Weight: 20%) — Score: 3 / 5
Light warm-grey occupies an interesting middle position — warm enough to appeal to buyers on the India spectrum who find pure cool grey too cold, but grey enough to satisfy the modern/Japandi segment. Not as strong as warm cream for mass market; not as dramatically modern as cool grey for design-forward. The warm undertone is the differentiator vs. stone-001 (purely cool grey).

### D3 · Veining Drama & Naturalism (Weight: 18%) — Score: 4 / 5
Multiple parallel flowing veins create a strong rhythmic visual — this is "travertine drama" as distinct from "marble drama." Where marble drama is about branching (stone-001, stone-004), travertine drama is about flow. The parallel white veins on warm grey create a meditative linear rhythm that is highly photogenic and spatially commanding. Slightly penalised for regularity of vein spacing.

### D4 · Surface Finish Quality (Weight: 12%) — Score: 4 / 5
Open-pore matte is the most premium finish choice in 2024-2027 — aligns with the Japandi movement, architect specification preference in Mumbai/Bengaluru/Pune, and the broader shift away from high-gloss. The pore texture adds tactile depth that differentiates this panel from every other option in the batch.

### D5 · Application Versatility (Weight: 10%) — Score: 4 / 5
The large slab format and neutral warm-grey tone give this panel the broadest premium application range in the batch. Works for TV walls, foyer, bedroom, dining feature, bathroom. Loses one point because the strong directional vein flow requires thoughtful installation planning.

### D6 · Tactile Texture (Weight: 8%) — Score: 4 / 5
Best tactile quality of all the grey/neutral panels. The open-pore surface creates genuine haptic differentiation from polished or semi-matte panels. The pore channels visible in the field suggest real travertine's natural porosity.

### D7 · Trend Alignment (Weight: 7%) — Score: 4 / 5
Travertine is the #1 stone design trend globally in 2024-2027, now fully established in premium Indian projects. This panel's material language (warm grey + flowing veins + open pore matte) is the definitive expression of the travertine aesthetic. Strong trend alignment.
