### Visual Distinctiveness
The most tactilely dominant panel in this batch. The "Classical Rock" label and rough 3D relief surface creates an entirely different product category from the marble-look panels — this is a **texture-first** surface that competes on haptic drama rather than veining. The off-white tone keeps it from being too rustic, bridging raw stone and clean contemporary. The shadow cast by the relief at different lighting angles is a major installation variable.

## B4 · Scoring — 7 Dimensions

### D1 · Design Fidelity (Weight: 25%) — Score: 3 / 5
Convincing as a rock-face / cleft stone texture but not referencing any specific prestigious marble. Fidelity is to a natural stone archetype rather than a named reference. The undulation pattern appears organic (not obviously mechanical repeat at this image scale). Penalised because the stone reference is vernacular rather than aspirational — this reads as sandstone/quartzite rather than a prestige material.

### D2 · Colour-Palette Fit (Weight: 20%) — Score: 3 / 5
Off-white cream is warm-tone aligned and broadly acceptable, but the texture effect dominates the colour read — the surface's value comes from relief, not from colour finesse. Works in Indian contexts where natural stone walls are a reference point (haveli, stone bungalows), but the muted cream doesn't generate the "premium marble" aspiration that drives purchase.

### D3 · Veining Drama & Naturalism (Weight: 18%) — Score: 1 / 5
No veining. The scoring system penalises heavily here because veining is the core driver of premium stone panel value. The panel compensates with texture, but D3 score reflects the absence of veining as a design element.

### D4 · Surface Finish Quality (Weight: 12%) — Score: 4 / 5
Matte finish is the right call for a rock-face textured surface — gloss would look artificial and highlight any tooling marks. The matte execution appears consistent. On-trend for 2024-2027.

### D5 · Application Versatility (Weight: 10%) — Score: 2 / 5
Heavy texture dramatically limits application range. Not suitable for: kitchen shutters, wardrobe fronts, bathroom tile, flooring. Best as: accent wall, foyer texture feature, exterior-interior transition zones, restaurant/hospitality raw-edge applications. Limited to feature wall roles only.

### D6 · Tactile Texture (Weight: 8%) — Score: 5 / 5
Perfect score. This is the highest-relief, most tactilely dominant panel in the batch. The 3D surface depth creates genuine haptic differentiation that flat-faced panels cannot replicate. Lighting-reactive — changes character dramatically between daytime and evening illumination. This is where the panel's premium case lives entirely.

### D7 · Trend Alignment (Weight: 7%) — Score: 4 / 5
Biophilic design and raw natural textures are a strong 2024-2027 trend in Indian urban interiors, particularly driven by design-forward millennials and boutique hospitality. The rough rock face is strongly aligned with this vector. Slightly penalised because the trend is segment-specific (design-forward, not mass market).
