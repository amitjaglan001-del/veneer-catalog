## Visual Profile

- **Base Tone:** Medium warm chocolate-brown — sits in the J. nigra/regia overlap zone. Warmer and lighter than NB016-3 (dark rift), richer and deeper than WN1517-3 (faded)
- **Grain Pattern:** Rift/quarter cut — predominantly straight with subtle organic wavering. Not mechanically parallel — has natural micro-irregularity that reads as real quarter-sawn timber
- **Tonal Variation:** Moderate — lighter and darker streaks along grain lines. Subtle tonal shifts create depth without drama
- **Pore:** Open, visible — this is the key differentiator. You can see individual pore marks across the surface, giving tactile depth even in the photo. Suggests either genuine EIR embossing or very high-resolution pore printing
- **Character:** Quiet, sophisticated, modern — the "whisper" name is apt. This is not loud walnut; it's considered walnut. Specification-grade aesthetic

## Film Mimicry Notes

- **Complexity:** Moderate-High — rift grain is geometrically simple but pore accuracy is demanding
- **EIR Status:** Appears to have genuine open-pore rendering — confirm under raking light
- **Key Challenge:** Maintaining visible pore at production scale; avoiding mechanical regularity in rift lines
- **Pattern Repeat:** 3.0m+ estimated — rift grain allows longer repeat than cathedral, major advantage for large walls
- **Pitfall:** Losing the pore detail in mass production; rift lines becoming too regular/mechanical
